## Variable Directionality
IMPORTANT: All variables must be expressed as quantities or qualities that can increase or decrease, without implying whether the change is positive or negative.

Good examples:
- "Water temperature" (can increase or decrease)
- "Fish abundance" (can increase or decrease)
- "Market demand" (can increase or decrease)

Bad examples:
- "Water quality" (too vague)
- "Fishery health" (too vague)
- "Sustainability" (too vague)

When identifying variables:
1. Focus on characteristics that can increase or decrease
2. Avoid terms that imply positive or negative outcomes
3. Use neutral language that allows for both increases and decreases
4. Include any relevant system characteristic that can fluctuate
