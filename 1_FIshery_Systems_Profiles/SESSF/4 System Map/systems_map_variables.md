# SESSF Systems Map Variables

## Stock Variables
| ID | Name | Definition |
|----|------|------------|
| V1 | Stock Biomass | The total weight or number of fish in a population, measured in tonnes or numbers. Critical for determining sustainable harvest levels and stock status. |
| V2 | Recruitment Rate | The rate at which young fish enter the fishable population, influenced by spawning success, larval survival, and environmental conditions. |
| V3 | Mortality Rate | The rate at which fish die from natural causes and fishing, including both target and non-target species mortality. |
| V6 | Growth Rate | The rate at which individual fish increase in size, affected by food availability, temperature, and other environmental conditions. |
| V12 | Species Distribution | The spatial and temporal distribution of fish populations, increasingly influenced by climate change and ocean warming. |
| V19 | Stock Recovery | The process of rebuilding depleted fish populations to sustainable levels, often measured against biological reference points. |
| V30 | Stock Status | The current condition of a fish stock relative to reference points (B20, B40, B48), indicating whether it is sustainably fished, overfished, or rebuilding. |
| V34 | Spatial Stock Depletion | The localized reduction in fish abundance due to concentrated fishing effort in specific areas. |
| V36 | Species Abundance | The number or biomass of fish present in a given area, influenced by both natural and human factors. |

## Technical Variables
| ID | Name | Definition |
|----|------|------------|
| V5 | Fishing Mortality | The proportion of fish removed by fishing activities, measured as a rate of removal from the population. |
| V7 | Catch Volume | The total amount of fish caught, including both retained and discarded catch, measured in tonnes. |
| V9 | Fleet Size | The number of active fishing vessels in the fishery, which has decreased by approximately 60% between 2000-2021. |
| V10 | Efficiency | The effectiveness of fishing operations in catching target species while minimizing bycatch and environmental impact. |
| V13 | Fishing Patterns | The spatial and temporal distribution of fishing effort, influenced by regulations, weather, and fish availability. |
| V15 | Fishing Effort | The amount of fishing activity, measured in days fished, hours trawled, hook-hours, or net-hours. |
| V20 | Technology Adoption | The uptake of new fishing technologies and methods, including electronic monitoring and gear modifications. |
| V21 | Catchability | The ease with which fish can be caught, influenced by gear type, fish behavior, and environmental conditions. |
| V28 | Illegal Catch | Unauthorized fishing activities, including exceeding quotas and fishing in closed areas. |
| V71 | Fishing Methods | The types of gear and techniques used, including trawl, Danish seine, longline, and gillnet operations. |
| V73 | Catch Per Unit Effort | The amount of catch obtained per unit of fishing effort, used as an index of stock abundance. |
| V74 | Discard Rates | The proportion of catch returned to sea, varying by sector and target species. |
| V75 | Gear Selectivity | The ability of fishing gear to target specific species or sizes while minimizing bycatch. |
| V76 | Vessel Capacity | The fishing power of individual vessels, measured by engine power and gross tonnage. |
| V77 | Electronic Monitoring | The use of cameras and sensors to monitor fishing activities and catch composition. |
| V78 | Spatial Management | The use of area-based management tools including closures and marine parks. |
| V79 | Observer Coverage | The proportion of fishing trips monitored by independent observers. |
| V80 | Harvest Strategy | The framework for setting catch limits and management measures based on stock status. |

## Environmental Variables
| ID | Name | Definition |
|----|------|------------|
| V11 | Ocean Temperature | The temperature of ocean waters, with surface temperatures having increased by 1.2°C since 1950 in the region. |
| V29 | Climate Change Impact | The effects of climate change on marine ecosystems, including warming, acidification, and changing current patterns. |
| V41 | Sea Surface Temperature | The temperature of the ocean surface layer, critical for species distribution and ecosystem productivity. |
| V42 | Bottom Temperature | The temperature at the ocean floor, particularly important for demersal species. |
| V43 | Chlorophyll-a | A measure of phytoplankton biomass and primary productivity in the ocean. |
| V44 | Southern Oscillation Index | A measure of El Niño/La Niña development and intensity, affecting ocean conditions. |
| V45 | Southern Annular Mode | An indicator of north-south movement of westerly winds, affecting storm patterns. |
| V46 | Diatom:Dinoflagellate Ratio | The ratio between major phytoplankton groups, indicating food web structure. |
| V47 | Ocean Acidification | The decrease in ocean pH due to CO2 absorption, affecting marine organisms. |
| V48 | Ocean Currents | The direction and strength of major ocean currents, including the East Australian Current. |
| V49 | Upwelling Intensity | The strength of nutrient-rich water upwelling events. |
| V50 | Oxygen Levels | The concentration of dissolved oxygen in seawater, declining with warming. |
| V51 | Extreme Weather Events | The frequency and intensity of storms, floods, and marine heatwaves. |
